Air Cargo Tracking

Air cargo tracking is a service that allows customers to monitor the status and location of their shipments transported by air. This service is crucial for ensuring that goods reach their destination on time and in good condition. The process involves several stages, including cargo acceptance, security screening, loading onto the aircraft, transit through various airports, and finally, delivery to the destination.

How It Works:

  • Booking: Customers book air cargo services, providing details about the shipment, including weight, dimensions, and destination.
  • Tracking Number: Upon booking, a unique Air Waybill (AWB) number is generated, which acts as a tracking number for the shipment
  • Real-Time Updates: The AWB number can be used on the tracking website to receive real-time updates on the cargo’s status, including when it’s loaded, in transit, and delivered.
  • Notifications: Customers may receive notifications at key milestones, such as when the cargo departs or arrives at a specific location.
